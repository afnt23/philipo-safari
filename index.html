Did You Know?

Tanzania — A Land of Wonders

One of the oldest inhabited lands on Earth, Tanzania is as rich in human history as it is in wildlife.

◈

Cradle of Humanity

Olduvai Gorge in the Ngorongoro area holds fossils of our earliest ancestors dating back 1.8 million years. Mary Leakey discovered footprints of hominids here — 3.6 million years old.

◈

120+ Languages

Tanzania is home to over 120 ethnic groups, each with its own language and traditions — held together in peace by Swahili, one of Africa's great lingua francas.

◈

Born from Unity

The name Tanzania was created in 1964 when mainland Tanganyika united with the island of Zanzibar. TANganyika + ZANzibar = TANZANIA. The country gained independence from Britain in 1961, led by Julius Nyerere.

◈

Wildlife Capital of Africa

Tanzania contains 25% of all of Africa's large mammals. The Serengeti — meaning 'endless plains' in Maasai — hosts the world's largest mammal migration and is a Seven Natural Wonders of Africa.

◈

Swahili: The Ocean Language

Swahili was born from centuries of trade between Bantu-speaking Africans and Arab, Persian, and Indian merchants along the coast. Its name comes from the Arabic 'sahil' — meaning shore.

◈

Tanzanite — Rarer Than Diamonds

Found only in a tiny area near Kilimanjaro, tanzanite is estimated to be 1,000 times rarer than diamonds. The world's entire supply could be exhausted within decades.

Planning Your Trip

Getting Here — Airports

Tanzania has two airports convenient for Philipo's territory:

Kilimanjaro International (JRO) — Recommended

Located between Arusha and Moshi, 45 min from Arusha. The main gateway for Northern Circuit safaris and Kilimanjaro. Direct international connections: Amsterdam (KLM), Doha (Qatar Airways), Addis Ababa (Ethiopian Airlines), Nairobi, Dar es Salaam, Istanbul (Turkish Airlines).

Arusha Airport (ARK)

Smaller domestic and charter airport right in Arusha. Convenient for connections from Dar es Salaam and internal Tanzania flights.

Philipo arranges all airport pickups and ground transfers.

Currency & Money

The official currency is the Tanzanian Shilling (TZS). USD is also widely accepted for safari, lodge, and tour payments.

  • Exchange currency locally in Arusha — forex bureaux and banks offer far better rates than airports or home countries.
  • ATMs are available in Arusha and accept Visa and Mastercard.
  • Credit cards accepted at most lodges and hotels.
  • Bring clean, unmarked USD bills (post-2009) for the best local exchange rates.
  • Tipping is customary — Philipo will advise on amounts for porters, drivers, and camp staff.
