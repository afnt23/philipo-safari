Tanzania's Wildlife Sanctuaries

15+ national parks and reserves, thousands of species, one extraordinary guide. From the iconic plains of the Serengeti to the remote wilderness of Ruaha.

The Parks

Where Philipo Will Take You

Every park can be combined with others, or with tribal and mountain experiences. Philipo tailors each journey to the season and your interests.

Northern Circuit

Serengeti National Park

The world's most famous wildlife reserve. Endless grass plains host the Great Migration — two million wildebeest, zebra and gazelle moving in an ancient cycle. Year-round big cat action, with lion prides, cheetah and leopard sightings daily.

Big Five Great Migration Year-round
Northern Circuit

Ngorongoro Conservation Area

The world's largest intact volcanic caldera — a natural amphitheatre sheltering the densest concentration of wildlife on Earth. Black rhino, elephant, lion and over 500 bird species in one breathtaking bowl. Also home to Maasai communities and Olduvai Gorge.

Black Rhino Crater Rim Year-round
Northern Circuit

Tarangire National Park

Famous for its enormous elephant herds and ancient baobab trees that look like they're drawn from a fairy tale. The Tarangire River attracts extraordinary concentrations of wildlife during the dry season. Python, oryx and fringe-eared oryx are specialties.

Elephants Baobabs Jun–Oct best
Northern Circuit

Lake Manyara National Park

Compact but sensational — famous for tree-climbing lions lounging in fever trees, and vast flocks of flamingos painting the lake pink. The soda lake and dense groundwater forest make this one of Tanzania's most atmospheric parks.

Flamingos Tree-climbing Lions Apr–Oct
Southern Circuit

Ruaha National Park

Tanzania's largest park and one of Africa's best-kept secrets. Remote and raw, Ruaha offers an experience that feels truly wild — enormous lion prides, wild dog packs, huge elephant herds, and virtually no other vehicles in sight.

Wild Dogs Off the beaten path Jun–Oct
Southern Circuit

Nyerere National Park (Selous)

Africa's largest protected area — larger than Switzerland. Nyerere is unique for its boat safaris along the Rufiji River, walking safaris, and fly-camping under the stars. Hippos, crocodiles, and the rare African wild dog flourish here.

Boat Safari Hippos Jun–Oct
Western Parks

Gombe & Mahale Mountains

Jane Goodall's chimpanzees. Gombe is the site of the world's longest-running wildlife research project. Trek through dense forest to find habituated chimpanzee communities. Mahale adds mountain scenery and a beach on the shores of Lake Tanganyika.

Chimpanzees Forests Jul–Oct
Northern Circuit

Arusha National Park

Right at Philipo's doorstep, Arusha NP offers extraordinary variety — colobus monkeys, giraffe, buffalo and zebra against the dramatic backdrop of Mt Meru. Flamingos on Momela Lakes. The perfect half-day or day trip before or after a longer safari.

Colobus Monkeys Mt Meru Views Year-round
Southern Circuit

Katavi National Park

Tanzania's most remote and least visited national park — for the true safari purist. During the dry season, hundreds of hippos compete for shrinking pools while thousands of buffalo roam the floodplains. Virtually no crowds, ever.

Hippos Untouched wilderness Jun–Oct
