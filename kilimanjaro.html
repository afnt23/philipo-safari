Kilimanjaro Routes

Choosing Your Path

Philipo will recommend the best route for your fitness level, schedule, and the experience you want. The longer the route, the higher the success rate.

Lemosho Route

7–8 days
Difficulty:

Philipo's most recommended route for first-timers. Approaches from the remote western side, crossing the vast Shira Plateau with dramatic scenery. Low foot traffic, excellent acclimatisation profile, and the highest summit success rate (~95%). Every serious trekker's first choice.

Best for: first-timers, those who want scenic variety and high success probability.

Machame Route

6–7 days
Difficulty:

The 'Whiskey Route' — Kili's most scenic and most popular. Steep, dramatic, and rewarding. The Machame approach winds through extraordinary ecological zones, from dense rainforest to the surreal ice-world of the summit. 80% success rate on 7 days, lower on 6.

Best for: fit hikers who want drama and scenery. Choose 7 days over 6 for safety.

Marangu Route

5–6 days
Difficulty:

The 'Coca-Cola Route' — the only route with hut accommodation (dormitory-style). More gentle gradients make this feel accessible, but the shorter itinerary means lower acclimatisation time and a summit success rate of only ~65%. Best chosen for its comfort, not its ease.

Best for: those who prefer sleeping in huts. Consider adding an extra night.

Rongai Route

6–7 days
Difficulty:

The only route approaching from the north (Kenya side). Drier and less crowded than southern routes. The wide, open moorland views are spectacular and the more gradual ascent suits those who prefer a less steep approach. Recommended during the rainy season.

Best for: dry-season hikers, those wanting a quieter experience.

Northern Circuit

9–10 days
Difficulty:

The longest route circles Kilimanjaro's entire northern face, offering perspectives seen by almost no other trekker. The extended duration means outstanding acclimatisation — the highest success rate of any route (~95%). For those who want the full, unhurried Kilimanjaro experience.

Best for: those who want the best possible chance of summit and maximum wilderness.

Umbwe Route

5–6 days
Difficulty:

The most direct and most demanding route on the mountain. Near-vertical sections through dense forest, very limited acclimatisation time. Not recommended for first-time Kili trekkers — this route is for experienced high-altitude trekkers who know their bodies and want a serious challenge.

Best for: experienced trekkers only. Discuss with Philipo before choosing this route.

What to Bring

Complete Gear List

Temperatures on Kilimanjaro range from 30°C at the gate to -20°C at the summit. Being properly equipped is the difference between summit and descent.

Upper Body Layers

  • Thermal base layer (x2)
  • Fleece mid-layer
  • Waterproof hardshell jacket
  • Summit down jacket (-20°C rated)
  • Sun hat & warm beanie
  • Balaclava
  • Liner gloves + summit mitts

Lower Body & Feet

  • Thermal leggings
  • Waterproof trekking trousers
  • Waterproof gaiters
  • Broken-in waterproof hiking boots
  • Wool/merino socks (x4–5 pairs)
  • Camp sandals or crocs

Pack & Equipment

  • 60–80L main pack (porter carries it)
  • 20–25L day pack (you carry this)
  • Sleeping bag rated to -10°C
  • Trekking poles (strongly recommended)
  • Headlamp + spare batteries
  • Water bottles or hydration bladder (3L)

Sun & Personal Care

  • Sunglasses (UV400 or glacier glasses)
  • High-factor sunscreen (SPF 50+)
  • Lip balm with SPF
  • Blister prevention & first-aid kit
  • Camera / action cam

Medication

  • Diamox (acetazolamide) — discuss with your doctor
  • Ibuprofen / paracetamol
  • Rehydration salts
  • Personal prescription medications

Provided by Philipo

  • All camping equipment & tents
  • Mess tent, chairs, tables
  • All meals on the mountain
  • Emergency oxygen
  • Pulse oximeters & first-aid kit
  • All permits & fees

Beyond Kilimanjaro

Other Great Peaks

Kilimanjaro is extraordinary — but Tanzania and the surrounding region offer other magnificent mountains, each with their own character and rewards.

4,562 m

Mount Meru

Tanzania's second-highest peak sits inside Arusha National Park. A dramatic 3–4 day trek through forest alive with colobus monkeys and buffalo, before an exposed crater rim walk with stunning Kilimanjaro views on clear days. An outstanding acclimatisation peak before Kilimanjaro — or a magnificent expedition in its own right.

3,417 m

Mount Hanang

Tanzania's fourth-highest peak, rising dramatically from the Rift Valley floor near Katesh. Very few tourists ever visit — which makes this a special off-the-beaten-path experience. The surrounding area is home to the Barabaig people, a fascinating nomadic pastoralist culture. A 2-day hike with big views over the Rift.

2,637 m

Mount Longido

A sacred Maasai mountain on the Kenya border, rising from the thornbush savanna. A 1–2 day hike with extraordinary views over both Kenya and Tanzania. Walking with Maasai guides through cattle country to the summit is itself a profound cultural experience. Perfect to combine with an Arusha area safari.

Combine the Mountains

Every mountain experience can be combined with wildlife safaris, cultural tribal visits, or both. Philipo designs seamless itineraries.

Kilimanjaro + Safari

Summit first, then celebrate on the plains. There is nothing quite like watching lions after standing on Uhuru Peak.

12–16 days total

Meru + Kilimanjaro

The ultimate acclimatisation strategy. Trek Meru first to adapt to altitude, then tackle Kilimanjaro with a significantly higher chance of success.

10–12 days total

Summit + Tribes

Trek with Chagga cultural visits on the slopes, then meet Maasai warriors or Hadzabe hunters in the valley. Mountain and culture as one journey.

9–12 days total
